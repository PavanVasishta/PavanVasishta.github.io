About Me

I am currently working as a Postdoctoral Researcher under the supervision of David Filliat at the U2IS Lab of ENSTA ParisTech. I am working on predicting behaviour of shared space users in an environment for Autonomous Vehicles.

My CV can be found here (Updated Mar. 2020)

I defended my PhD in September 2019. Previously, I was a PhD Candidate in the CHROMA team at Inria Grenoble under the supervision of Anne Spalanzani and Dominique Vaufreydaz. During my PhD, I worked on understanding and predicting pedestrian behaviour around autonomous vehicles in built urban areas by building and exploiting prior knowledge. My research focused on using sociological ideas like Natural Vision as a basis for using probabilistic methods to predict pedestrian behaviour in urban areas. In this, I was aided by an ANR (Agence National de Recherche) grant for a collaborative project involving the research labs Inria Grenoble , Inria Paris, LS2N and the industrial partner AKKA.

I completed my MSc from the Ecole Centrale de Nantes in Advanced Robotics and have worke on making collaborative robots safer around human operators as an intern at IRT Jules Verne.

My current research interests include potential fields, Natural Vision, Markov models, self-organizing topological maps, pedestrian prediction in urban areas and decision making under uncertainity.
